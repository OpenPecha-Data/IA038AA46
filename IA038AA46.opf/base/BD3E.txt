PREFACE
'Phren-po Gter-chen ses-rab-'od-zer alias 'Gro-'dul-glin-pa (1518-1584) was one of the
most revered masters of the Rñin-ma-pa tradition. Aside from his treatise on the post-final-s in
Tibetan orthography and the few works included in the Rin chen gter mdzod, his works have
remained little known to scholars. Lopon Sonam Sangpo has graciously made available a rare
manuscript from his collection containing a number of the works of this great savant.
The gter-ston was born in the Byan Myriarchy at Nom-chen as a reembodiment of the
great Vairocana. He studied with Rdor-rgyal-ba (a disciple of Gser-mdog Pan-chen ſākya-mchog-
-Idan) and the Dga'-ldan Khri Rin-po.che Bstan-dar-ba. At the age of 18 he met the 'Bri-gun-pa
Bka’-brgyud-pa masier Rin-chen-phun-tshogs from whom he received the teachings of the 'Bri-gun-
-pa and Kam-tshan-pa traditions.
He spent almost a decade in contemplation at Grogs-ri and manifested numerous
signs of esoteric realization. In 1546 he was favoured by a vision of dākiņi who revealed to him
the prophecy of his discovery of the Grol tig Dgons pa ran grol cycle.
Under the patronage of Bsod-nams-stobs-rgyal of the 'Phyons-rgyas family, he established
Dpal-ri Theg-chen-glin. The chief heir to his teachings was his son Karma-kun-bzan who is the
author of a full length biography which has not yet come to light,
The present manuscript is missing a number of sections among which could be the
famous Żal lun gsal sgron praised by 'Jam-mgon Kon-sprul.
